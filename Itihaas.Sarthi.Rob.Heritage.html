India's Heritage

Itihaas Sarthi arrives preloaded with curated knowledge about some of India's most iconic cultural landmarks — ready to narrate, educate, and inspire every visitor.

Preloaded heritage sites

Each site in Itihaas Sarthi's memory is packed with historical facts, architectural details, cultural context, and stories — delivered in the visitor's chosen language the moment they arrive at the site.

01Taj MahalAgra, Uttar PradeshBuilt between 1631 and 1648 by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, the Taj Mahal is widely considered the finest example of Mughal architecture. Its symmetrical gardens, marble minarets, and central dome make it one of the world's most visited monuments and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
MughalUNESCO17th Century
02Qutub MinarMehrauli, New DelhiStanding 72.5 metres tall, the Qutub Minar is the world's tallest brick minaret. Construction began in 1193 under Qutb ud-Din Aibak and was completed by his successors. Surrounded by ruins of the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque and the enigmatic Iron Pillar, the complex represents the beginning of Islamic rule in India.
Delhi SultanateUNESCO12th Century
03HampiVijayanagara, KarnatakaOnce the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire — one of the greatest Hindu kingdoms in Indian history — Hampi flourished from the 14th to the 16th century. Today its vast ruins include over 1,600 surviving structures including temples, markets, and royal enclosures spread across a dramatic boulder-strewn landscape.
VijayanagaraUNESCO14th Century
04Konark Sun TempleKonark, OdishaBuilt in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga dynasty, the Konark Sun Temple is designed as a giant stone chariot of the Sun God Surya. Its 24 intricately carved wheels are astronomical instruments that track time, and its walls are covered in extraordinary erotic, devotional, and everyday-life sculptures.
Eastern GangaUNESCO13th Century
05Ajanta CavesAurangabad, MaharashtraCarved between the 2nd century BCE and 6th century CE, the 30 rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments at Ajanta contain some of the finest surviving examples of ancient Indian art. The murals and sculptures depict the life of the Buddha across multiple Jataka tales with extraordinary detail and emotion.
BuddhistUNESCO2nd Century BCE
06Ellora CavesAurangabad, MaharashtraDating from 600–1000 CE, Ellora's 34 caves represent three faiths — Buddhism, Hinduism, and Jainism — side by side. The Kailasa Temple (Cave 16), carved entirely from a single basalt cliff, is the largest monolithic rock excavation in the world and a remarkable feat of ancient engineering.
Multi-faithUNESCO7th–10th Century
