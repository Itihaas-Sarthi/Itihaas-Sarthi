What Is It

A robot built for India's heritage

Itihaas Sarthi is an autonomous guiding robot designed to navigate heritage sites, narrate cultural history through audio in multiple languages, and physically assist visually impaired visitors with a retractable handle system. It combines GPS, sensors, a camera, and onboard knowledge to deliver a seamless, educational experience.

How It Works

From activation to narration

01
RFID Activation The robot wakes up when an authorised RFID tag is scanned, ready to assist.
02
Location Detection GPS and camera identify the exact heritage site and surrounding monuments.
03
Facts Retrieved Onboard memory serves curated, accurate cultural information about the site.
04
Audio Narration Text-to-speech speaks history aloud in the visitor's chosen language.
05
Guided Movement Ultrasonic sensors and cliff detectors navigate safely, vibrating to warn of obstacles.

Features

Built for everyone, everywhere

Accessibility First A retractable handle extends automatically for visually impaired visitors, guiding them safely through the site with vibration alerts for turns and obstacles.
Multilingual Narration Speaks cultural facts in Hindi, English, and regional languages — so every visitor connects with history in their own tongue.
GPS-Powered Context Knows exactly where it is and automatically loads the right historical facts — no manual input needed from the visitor.
Camera Vision Onboard camera identifies monuments in real time, enriching narration with visual recognition of the surrounding environment.
Storage Compartment Carries visitor essentials — water bottles, bags, and belongings — so tourists can explore hands-free and comfortably.
Safe & Reliable Power A rechargeable Li-ion battery with a built-in management system keeps the robot running all day, safely and efficiently.
