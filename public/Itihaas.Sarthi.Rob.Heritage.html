India's Heritage

Itihaas Sarthi arrives preloaded with curated knowledge about some of India's most iconic cultural landmarks — ready to narrate, educate, and inspire.

Each site in Itihaas Sarthi's memory is packed with historical facts, architectural details, cultural context, and stories — delivered in the visitor's chosen language the moment they arrive.

01 Taj Mahal Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of Mumtaz Mahal, this UNESCO World Heritage Site in Agra is one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture.
02 Qutub Minar A 73-metre minaret in Delhi built in 1193, the Qutub Minar is the tallest brick minaret in the world and a remarkable example of early Indo-Islamic architecture.
03 Hampi The ruins of Hampi in Karnataka were once the prosperous capital of the Vijayanagara Empire — a vast site of temples, markets, and royal complexes dating to the 14th century.
04 Konark Sun Temple Built in the 13th century in Odisha, this magnificent temple is designed as a colossal chariot of the Sun God, adorned with intricate stone carvings and sculptures.
05 Ajanta & Ellora Caves These ancient rock-cut cave complexes in Maharashtra feature stunning Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain art and architecture spanning nearly a thousand years of Indian history.
