Our Mission

Why Itihaas Sarthi exists — and what it aims to achieve for India's cultural heritage and its visitors.

The Why

Making heritage accessible to all

India has over 3,600 centrally protected monuments and thousands more state-protected heritage sites. Yet the majority of these sites lack audio guides, accessible pathways for the differently abled, or multilingual signage. The average visitor leaves knowing very little more than they arrived with.

Itihaas Sarthi was created to change that. The goal is a robot that any visitor — regardless of language, ability, or prior knowledge — can use to experience India's history the way it deserves to be experienced: vividly, accurately, and personally.

Core Goals

What we are building towards

Every design decision in Itihaas Sarthi is guided by three commitments:

01 — Accessibility

No visitor should be left behind. Whether visually impaired, hearing impaired, or simply unfamiliar with the local language — Itihaas Sarthi must serve them all with equal depth and care.

02 — Accuracy

Historical facts must be correct, sourced from credible records, and presented without sensationalism. Itihaas Sarthi should be a tool for education, not entertainment at history's expense.

03 — Autonomy

The robot must work independently — no internet, no operator assistance, no charging stops mid-tour. Visitors at remote sites deserve the same experience as those at well-resourced monuments.
