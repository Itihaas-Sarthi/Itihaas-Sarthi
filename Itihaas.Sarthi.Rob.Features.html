Features

Everything Itihaas Sarthi can do — designed for India's heritage environments and built for all kinds of visitors.

Accessibility

Helping every visitor

Retractable HandleA telescopic handle extends automatically when a user is detected via touch sensor. It provides a stable physical guide for visually impaired visitors, with textured grip and Braille markings for orientation. A push button allows manual extension and retraction at any time.
Vibration AlertsA vibration motor sends distinct pulses before turns, when approaching obstacles, and at emergency stops. Different patterns signal different events — giving users a clear, non-visual warning system throughout the tour.
Emergency StopA large, tactile emergency stop button immediately halts all robot movement. It is oversized and raised for easy access by any user, and located prominently on the robot's body for fast response in any situation.

Knowledge & Audio

Bringing history to life

Multilingual NarrationText-to-speech narrates historical facts in Hindi, English, and regional Indian languages. Language is changed with a single tactile button. Volume is independently controllable, ensuring clear audio in outdoor environments and quiet museum spaces alike.
GPS-Powered ContextThe GPS module identifies the visitor's location and auto-selects the correct site knowledge. No manual input is required. An optional NFC or QR reader further refines content at individual monument level within large complexes.
Camera VisionAn onboard camera allows the robot to visually identify its surroundings in real time. It enriches narration with monument recognition and provides additional spatial awareness to complement the ultrasonic sensor system.

Navigation & Safety

Moving safely through any site

Obstacle DetectionAn ultrasonic sensor mounted in an open Technic frame scans 180° ahead, detecting walls, people, and monuments. Readings are processed every 100ms to ensure real-time responsiveness in crowded heritage sites.
Cliff DetectionAn IR cliff sensor continuously checks for sudden drops, steps, and ledges. This is critical for protecting both the robot and visually impaired visitors near stairways, raised platforms, and uneven terrain common at outdoor heritage sites.
Long-Life BatteryA 7.4V Li-ion battery pack powers all systems for a full day of operation. The built-in Battery Management System prevents overcharging and overheating. A power switch and charging port make end-of-day recharging simple and fast.

Practical Design

Built for real-world use

Storage CompartmentA dedicated compartment inside the robot's chassis holds visitor belongings — water bottles, bags, cameras, and personal items — so tourists can explore entirely hands-free and comfortably.
Weather-Resistant CasingThe robot's outer casing is designed to withstand outdoor conditions at exposed heritage sites — dust, humidity, and light rain. Sensors are mounted in open Lego Technic frames to ensure no transparent material blocks their readings.
Modular Lego Technic FrameMoving parts including arms and structural joints are built using Lego Technic components — 1×19 frames, bent beams, and A-shape frames — making the robot easy to modify, repair, and upgrade over time.
