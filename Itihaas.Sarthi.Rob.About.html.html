About the Robot

Itihaas Sarthi is an autonomous guiding robot built from the ground up to preserve, narrate, and bring Indian heritage to life — for every kind of visitor.

The Project

Built for India's heritage

Itihaas Sarthi is a fully self-contained robot — no internet required at the site. It runs onboard GPS, preloaded historical data, a camera, and an audio system to guide visitors autonomously through heritage sites across India. It is designed from the ground up to serve all visitors, including those who are visually impaired, by providing a retractable physical handle alongside spoken navigation instructions.

Accessibility First

A telescopic handle extends for visually impaired users. Vibration motors pulse at turns and near obstacles, acting as a tactile alert system alongside spoken audio.

Multilingual Narration

Speaks historical facts in Hindi, English, and regional languages. A tactile language selector button lets any visitor choose their preferred language in seconds.

Safe Autonomous Navigation

Four wheels driven by servo motors navigate outdoor and indoor heritage environments. Ultrasonic and cliff sensors prevent collisions and falls at steps or ledges.

Storage Compartment

A built-in compartment holds visitor belongings — water bottles, bags, and personal items — so guests can explore completely hands-free.
